From the Founders

A note on why we began
“

The Gavel Gazette began with a simple belief: legal ideas should not remain locked inside classrooms, casebooks, or courtrooms. They should be discussed, questioned, and understood by the people they affect.

We created this publication as a space for disciplined student journalism — where research meets storytelling, where legal developments are explained with care, and where every piece begins with responsibility to the reader.

— The Founding Editorial Board
